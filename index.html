Story 01 · Chaffey College · Inland Empire / Desert

Ryan Hitch

Instructional Specialist, Distance Education

He had to move an entire faculty toward Universal Design for Learning — one 60-minute workshop at a time.

"The more problems a tool solves at once, the more indispensable it becomes."Ryan's design principle — "utility stacking"

So Ryan built the UDL Assessment Bot to carry the load between sessions. Faculty upload a course outline — low-stakes — and it returns UDL-aligned assessment ideas, then quietly maps each one to Chaffey's ACES skills framework. Hesitant faculty see it work before they're ever asked to share more.

That sequencing is the design. The bot solves two problems at once — better assessments and ACES alignment — and that's what makes faculty come back.

The app — the root

UDL Assessment Bot

Generates UDL-aligned assessments from a course outline, mapped to a transferable-skills framework, with a trust-ramp that proves value before asking for more.

Story 02 · Cuyamaca College · San Diego & Imperial

My-Linh Nguyen

Professor, Counselor & Counseling Dept Chair, Ph.D.

Five counselors. Nine thousand students. A ratio of 1,317 to 1 — and she read it as a reason to build.

"We seriously had nothing before this."My-Linh, on the gap her probation bot now fills

A self-described AI skeptic, My-Linh changed her mind when she realized a "chatbot" could be the interactive FAQ she'd always wanted. She remixed a Santa Monica colleague's major-and-career generator, retuned the questions with Cuyamaca's career counselor, and mapped it to Cuyamaca's own catalog of majors and certificates.

Her companion probation bot answers students in their own languages, at any hour — replacing what used to be a dead-end webpage of policy jargon.

The app — the root

Cuyamaca Major / Pathway Generator

A career-interest assessment that routes students to real programs, with edge-case logic for undecided and transfer students — AB928 / ADT alignment built in.

Story 03 · Cuyamaca College · San Diego & Imperial

Khrystyn Pamintuan

General, International & Embedded Counselor — Behavioral & Social Sciences

Down the hall from My-Linh, the same impossible math — and a different answer.

"What kind of personality and values do I want this tool to embody?"Khrystyn's first design question

With her department chair, Khrystyn built Ask CeCe — a conversational counseling assistant that replaced a static FAQ dropdown and answers students 24/7. CeCe is built with a boundary: she knows she isn't a counselor, and hands students to a human when it matters. That honesty is what won the department's trust.

Now Khrystyn is building population-specific assistants — for international students, and for Social Work and Wellness Coach students — an ecosystem that protects her time for the conversations only a counselor can have.

The app — the root

Ask CeCe

A 24/7 counseling-FAQ assistant on the counseling webpage, built from a strengths-based, equity-minded lens — and honest about its limits, referring students to a human counselor when needed.

Lives on Cuyamaca's counseling page

Growing into a suite: program-specific & international-student assistants.

Story 04 · Foothill College · Bay Area

Sally Baldwin

Health Instructor & Faculty Support Champion

Faculty were losing hours to accessibility, AI-policy, and accreditation questions. She saw a pattern.

"People often don't need more information. They need help turning information into decisions."Sally's design philosophy

Sally's answer wasn't an app — it was a responsible-AI ecosystem: a policy generator, a use labeler, and a student disclosure tool, built to work together and embedded in Foothill's AI Hub and faculty handbook.

Build the workflow before the technology, she says. Put the tools where people already work, and they get discovered, used, and maintained.

The app — the root

Responsible-AI Ecosystem

Three interlocking tools — AI Policy Generator, AI Use Labeler, and My AI Process & Disclosure — designed as one workflow, not three separate bots.

Lives in Foothill's AI Hub

Affordability siblings: ZTC Program Finder & Zero-Cost Degree Bot.

Story 05 · Hartnell College · South Central Coast

Jacob Jun

Project Facilitator (ITR) & Technologist

Students show up at the IT desk with questions that were never about IT.

"When students hit a dead end, they don't just lose time. They lose momentum."Jacob, on why navigation is an equity issue

Jacob built OscarBot, a student-facing assistant that answers technical questions and — just as important — points students to the right place when the answer lives somewhere else on campus. It reads from Hartnell's website and casts wider for technical questions.

It won't always have the exact answer. But for first-generation students facing a complex institution, a starting point beats a dead end. Know your resources, Jacob says, and design from the student's perspective.

The app — the root

OscarBot

A student-facing front door that answers tech questions and routes everything else to the right campus resource — meeting students who don't yet know where to start.

The bigger picture

Five people. Five colleges. One system learning to change itself.

None of these started as software projects. They started with a teacher, a counselor, a technologist noticing something break — and deciding to fix it. Each built a root that others can adapt. At Cuyamaca, two counselors have already turned individual tools into a shared collection. That's the pattern, in miniature.

People → stories → apps → lineages → collections. Multiply by 661 educators and 1,900+ apps across 116 colleges, and the library stops being a library. It becomes how the system changes itself — from the inside.

That's the real artifact the AI Solutions Studio is built to hold: not an inventory of apps, but a record of distributed changemaking — and a map others can follow.
